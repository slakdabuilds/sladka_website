# Sladka Brand Guidelines

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Brand name:** Sladka

**Tagline:** AI Growth Consultancy

**Extended line:** Helping businesses grow using AI, strategy, and intelligent systems.

**One-sentence brand description:**
Sladka is an AI growth consultancy that helps modern businesses scale using strategy, automation, and intelligent systems.

**Brand positioning:**
Sladka operates as a consulting-led firm, not a traditional marketing agency. The company advises businesses on growth strategy and AI adoption, then designs and builds the systems needed to execute those strategies.
> Consulting → Strategy → Systems → Implementation

**Brand personality:**
Authoritative · Minimal · Modern · Intellectual · Strategic · Understated · Exclusive · Analytical · Calm confidence · Systems-oriented · Thoughtful

**Tone of voice:**
Writing should feel clear, intelligent, and composed — like a strategic briefing rather than marketing copy. Avoid hype and exaggeration. Confidence should come from clarity and insight, never from superlatives.

**Words / phrases to NEVER use:**
synergy, disruption, game-changer, unlock potential, leverage (as marketing jargon), cutting-edge, best-in-class, empower, revolutionary, transformative (overused), seamless

## 2. Colour System

The colour system is designed to feel calm, premium, and strategic — similar to modern consulting and research firms.

### Signature Brand Colour
**Muted Sage Teal — `#91AAA0`**
The defining Sladka colour. Use for buttons, highlights, charts, UI accents, section dividers, and key callouts.

### Full Palette

| Role | Hex | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| Signature / Accent | `#91AAA0` | Brand accent, highlights, icons |
| Primary Neutral (BG) | `#F6F4F0` | Main page background |
| Secondary Neutral | `#E9E7E3` | Section backgrounds |
| Soft Neutral | `#CFC8BE` | UI surfaces, subtle fills |
| Dark Text | `#1A1A1A` | Headlines |
| Body Text | `#4B4B4B` | Paragraph copy |
| Muted Text | `#8E8E8E` | Labels, captions, dates |
| Card Background | `#ECE8E2` | Individual cards |
| Divider | `#DED9D2` | Borders, horizontal rules |

### Colour Usage Rules
Maintain a predominantly neutral palette:
- **70%** neutral tones (`#F6F4F0`, `#E9E7E3`)
- **20%** soft neutrals (`#CFC8BE`, `#ECE8E2`)
- **10%** signature colour (`#91AAA0`)

Use the signature colour for: buttons, links, chart elements, icons, key UI accents, left-border card accents.
Never use the signature colour as a full-page or hero background.

## 3. Typography

Typography should feel modern, clean, and highly readable. Inter is the primary font across all weights.

**Headline font:** Inter · Weight 600–700 · Sentence case · Never all caps · Generous spacing

**Subheading font:** Inter · Weight 500

**Body font:** Inter · Weight 400 · Line height 1.6

**Label / caption font:** Inter · Small caps or uppercase · Slight letter spacing (0.12–0.18em)

**Email fallback fonts:** Arial, Helvetica

**Typography rules:**
- Headlines: sentence case always, never all caps
- Body line height: 1.6–1.75
- No decorative or script fonts for English content
- ⚠️ NOTE: Gulzar / Madani are reserved for Arabic-language content only (see Section 11)

## 5. Newsletter Layout

The newsletter aesthetic should resemble a modern strategy briefing — minimal, editorial, data-driven. Inspired by consulting research publications.

**Overall aesthetic:** Minimal · Editorial · Data-driven
Reference: McKinsey research decks, The Economist, FT Weekend

**Hero section:**
- Background: light neutral (`#F6F4F0`)
- Optional category label/chip above headline
- Headline: large Inter 600–700, dark (`#1A1A1A`)

**Section dividers:** Whitespace with a subtle divider line (`#DED9D2`)

**Sections to ALWAYS include:**
- Key facts / bullet summaries
- Statistics / key numbers
- Infographics
- Sources

**Sections to NEVER include:**
- Casual storytelling
- Opinionated commentary
- Personal anecdotes

**Card style:** Subtle border (`#DED9D2`) · Neutral fill (`#ECE8E2`) · Signature colour left-border accent

**Footer:** Light background · Include: Sladka name · Issue date · Tagline

## 6. Infographic Style (Nanobanana)

Infographics should look like they belong in a McKinsey or Financial Times research deck. Clean, structured, minimal, authoritative.

**Overall aesthetic prompt language:**
> "Clean consulting-style editorial infographic. Strong grid layout, generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, minimal decoration. Inspired by McKinsey research decks and The Economist. Muted sage teal (#91AAA0) accents on a warm off-white background (#F6F4F0). Dark charcoal (#1A1A1A) headlines in clean modern sans-serif. Never cluttered, no gradients, no cartoon icons, no neon colours. Professional and calm."

**Background:** Light neutral (`#F6F4F0`) preferred

**Infographic colour palette:**

| Role | Hex |
|------|-----|
| Primary accent | `#91AAA0` |
| Secondary | `#5A5F4C` |
| Background | `#F6F4F0` |
| Text (on light) | `#1A1A1A` |
| Muted text | `#8E8E8E` |

**Typography in infographics:**
- Headlines: bold, clean sans-serif, dark (`#1A1A1A`)
- Labels: small uppercase, muted tone (`#8E8E8E`), letter-spaced
- Numbers/stats: large, bold, signature colour (`#91AAA0`) or dark

**NEVER include:**
Neon colours · Cartoon or clipart icons · Gradients · Cluttered layouts · Photorealistic people · 3D effects · Drop shadows (heavy)

**ALWAYS include:**
Clean grid · Thin border accents · Generous whitespace · Clear visual hierarchy · Professional editorial feel

**Reference aesthetic:** McKinsey Insights · The Economist · Financial Times · Bloomberg Businessweek

## 8. Content Rules

**Preferred length:** Medium (~100 words)

**Always cite sources:** Yes

**Fact density:** Medium to high — clear insights supported by data

**Perspective / POV:** Advisory — writing should feel like guidance for business leaders

**Topics we cover:**
AI for business · Automation · Systems thinking · Growth strategy · Digital transformation · Modern marketing infrastructure

**Topics we never cover:**
Politics · Gossip · Sensational news · General lifestyle content

**Audience:**
Business owners, founders, decision-makers. Intelligent and time-constrained. Skip basic explanations — don't over-explain simple ideas. Write as if briefing a smart executive.

## 9. Sample Headlines

These set the tone and voice for all newsletter headlines:

1. AI Will Replace Most Marketing Agencies. Here's What Survives.
2. Why Restaurants Are Quietly Replacing Marketing Teams With AI.
3. The Hidden Cost of Running a Business Without Automation.
4. Most Businesses Still Operate Like It's 2015.
5. Why AI Systems Will Replace Traditional Consulting.

**Headline style notes:**
- Direct, declarative, confident
- No question headlines unless genuinely provocative
- Short sentences or two-part structure (statement · follow-up)
- Never cute or clever — always substantive

## 11. Arabic Language

All newsletters are English by default. Arabic newsletters are only produced when explicitly requested.

When an Arabic newsletter is requested:
- Full RTL layout
- All fonts switch to Madani (headlines and body)
- Gulzar is NOT used
- Inter is NOT used for Arabic text
- Same colour system and layout structure applies
